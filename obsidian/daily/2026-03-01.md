## Key Decisions Made
- Safety checker now does proactive lab safety checks on ALL existing medications, not just GDMT proposals. This catches Patient 003's dangerous K+ 5.1 (hold spironolactone) and creatinine rise (hold lisinopril) even though GDMT only evaluates diuretics and beta blockers.
- Used a 30-day window for proactive creatinine rise detection (vs 14-day for proposed changes) to catch ongoing renal decline after medication changes.
- Validator checks for three violation types: unknown drug names, unknown doses, and hyphens in patient text.
- Pipeline runs in strict fixed order: adherence > trajectory > GDMT > safety > barriers > escalation.
- Barrier planner only evaluates safe changes (filters out blocked packets before passing to barriers).

## Problems Encountered
- Initial safety checker only evaluated proposed changes, missing critical lab-based safety issues on existing medications (Patient 003 K+ 5.1 on spironolactone was not flagged).
- Creatinine rise detection used 14-day window which missed Patient 003's lisinopril issue (changed 27 days ago). Fixed with broader 30-day window for proactive checks.
- `python` command not available, had to use `python3` on macOS.

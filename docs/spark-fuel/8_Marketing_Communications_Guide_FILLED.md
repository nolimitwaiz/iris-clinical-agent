## 1. Brand Positioning

### One Sentence Positioning
Iris is the AI care agent that monitors heart failure patients 24/7 using guideline based clinical tools, not language model guessing — so clinicians can trust every recommendation and patients feel heard between visits.

### Tagline Options
1. "Guideline driven care, always on."
2. "Every recommendation has a citation."
3. "Your care team, extended to 24/7."

### Brand Personality
- **Warm but clinical** — Iris speaks to patients like a knowledgeable, caring nurse, not a robot or a doctor
- **Transparent** — every decision is traceable and auditable
- **Humble** — Iris does not replace clinicians, it extends their reach
- **Accessible** — designed for elderly patients with limited tech literacy, not Silicon Valley early adopters

### Brand Voice Guidelines
- Use plain language. Say "water pill" alongside "diuretic"
- Never claim to replace doctors — always position as extending the care team
- Lead with patient outcomes, not technology
- Avoid AI hype language ("revolutionary," "breakthrough," "powered by advanced AI")
- Use specific numbers: "caught a 2 lb weight gain on day 3" not "monitors patients continuously"
- No hyphens in patient facing text (style requirement)

## 2. Customer Journey Map

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Who:** HF program directors, nurse managers, CMOs
**Where they learn about new tools:** ACC and HFSA annual meetings, peer publications, hospital quality committee discussions, LinkedIn clinical groups
**What they are thinking:** "Our readmission rate is too high and we don't have enough nurses to run our RPM program properly"
**Iris touchpoint:** Conference presentation showing pilot data, LinkedIn article about tool first AI architecture, peer referral from Hopkins pilot site

### Stage 2: Interest
**What they are thinking:** "This sounds different from other RPM platforms — no nurses needed for routine monitoring? I want to understand how it works"
**Iris touchpoint:** Product demo (live or recorded), one pager comparing Iris to existing RPM workflow, case study from pilot site
**Key message:** "Every recommendation traces to a published guideline. Your clinicians review and approve. The AI never decides alone."

### Stage 3: Evaluation
**What they are thinking:** "Can I trust this? What does my IT team think? What does the cardiologist think? What is the ROI?"
**Iris touchpoint:** Clinician facing deep dive (show Action Packets, audit trail, Response Validator), ROI calculator customized to their CMS penalty exposure, IT security review (runs locally, FHIR export, no PHI in cloud by default)
**Key message:** "Start with 50 patients for 6 months. If readmissions don't drop, walk away."

### Stage 4: Decision
**What they are thinking:** "I need to get buy in from the department chief and CMO. I need to justify this to the quality committee"
**Iris touchpoint:** Board ready slide deck with ROI projections, clinical advisory board endorsements, published pilot results, reference calls with pilot site clinicians
**Key message:** "At $75/patient/month, you prevent 1 readmission per 15 patients. That is a 3x return before counting CMS penalty reduction."

### Stage 5: Adoption
**What they are thinking:** "How fast can we get this running? Will our nurses actually use it? Will patients engage?"
**Iris touchpoint:** 2 week onboarding program, dedicated implementation support, nurse training session (1 hour), patient enrollment via voice onboarding (no app download needed)
**Key message:** "Patients just talk. Nurses just review Action Packets. No new workflow to learn."

### Stage 6: Advocacy
**What they are thinking:** "This is working — our readmission rate is down and nurses are happier. Other departments are asking about it"
**Iris touchpoint:** Quarterly outcomes report, support for conference abstract submission, co authored case study publication, expansion to additional patient panels
**Key message:** "Your results are worth sharing. We will help you publish."

## 5. Content Strategy

### Content Pillars

1. **Clinical credibility** — content demonstrating that Iris follows guidelines, not guesses
   - "How Iris follows AHA/ACC 7.3.2 for diuretic adjustment" (blog/LinkedIn)
   - Action Packet walkthrough video
   - Concordance study results (Iris vs cardiologist recommendations)

2. **Patient stories** — showing real impact on real people
   - "How a 2 lb weight gain was caught before it became an ER visit" (case vignette)
   - Patient testimonial video (with consent)
   - Family view demo: "What caregivers see when Mom uses Iris"

3. **Health system ROI** — financial case for adoption
   - ROI calculator (interactive web tool)
   - "What $800K in CMS penalties could buy instead" (infographic)
   - Pilot results: readmission reduction numbers with before/after comparison

4. **Architecture transparency** — building trust through openness
   - "Why we do not let the AI make clinical decisions" (thought leadership)

   - Technical deep dive for health system IT evaluators
